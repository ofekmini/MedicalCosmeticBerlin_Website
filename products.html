The products I use

& A bit of myths breaking

I carefully selected the products I use at the clinic. Every company I choose has an agenda similar to mine - they all strive for a perfect combination of natural raw products, to maximize their capabilities with the help of science.
The ingredients I choose do not contain mineral oils or any chemical waste that has become useful in the cosmetics industry. On the other hand, I am also not in favor of non-synthesized products like raw products in their natural form (such as food or natural oils).
Don’t get me wrong - I am in favor of eating avocado for example, but less in favor of spreading it on the face. This can be nice for skins that do not suffer from problems, maybe the liquids in it can provide a soft and moisturizing look for a short time, but in practice, studies show that none of the vitamins or minerals of avocado or any other fruit / vegetable spreading on the skin can penetrate it.
It also important to know that in addition to vitamins and minerals, fruits/vegetables also have substances that turn dirt on the skin, for example the sugary liquids in them or the fibers.
Another myth I can break about homemade masks: the use of egg.
Many recipes for homemade masks include egg.
The egg is mostly made of proteins, so when it is applied to the skin and dries, a stretching effect is created. This effect lasts for one to several hours. After a few hours the protein will peel off and give the appearance of dry and flaky skin.
Briefly I will say that sometimes it’s fun to make homemade masks.
But for serious skin problems and proper results-
The first important thing is precisely match your skin type to the product by a professional.
And my next advice: it’s better to invest in product from a quality trustable company, which has serious laboratory and proven results.
